# ⚡ Lightning Talk Outline: "How Devs Use AI"
**Based on GitKraken's "The AI Multiplier Effect" (2,172 developer-weeks of data)**

---

## Intro
- **Who am I?**

---

## Why this talk?
- GitKraken, GitClear, and GitKraken Insights conducted a study, "Analysis of 2,172 developer-weeks across teams using GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and Claude Code"
- NOT A PRODUCT PITCH

---

## HOOK — "The Number Everyone Quotes Is Wrong" *(~45 sec)*
- You've heard it: "AI makes devs 10x more productive"
- The data *does* show power users with 14x more commits, 15x more test code
- **But here's the problem:** that number is almost entirely misleading
- Tease: the real number is smaller — and honestly, more impressive

---

## WHO ACTUALLY USES AI HEAVILY? *(~60 sec)*
- It's not random — AI power users cluster around:
  - **Senior engineers** (already touch more of the codebase)
  - **Startup devs** (less process friction, nearly 2x output vs. enterprise)
  - **Devs in flow states** (AI is the companion to high output, maybe not the cause)
- Key insight: **AI amplifies what's already there.** It doesn't create 10x engineers from scratch.

---

## THE REAL NUMBER *(~60 sec)*
- Researchers compared devs against *themselves* — same person, same week, one year apart
- Result: regular AI users are **~25% more productive** year-over-year
- Genuine wins:
  - **4x more test code** written (friction drops → devs actually write tests)
  - AI-assisted PRs need **less review time per unit of output**
- 25% is real. It compounds. That's actually a big deal.

---

## THE CATCH — WHAT AI COSTS YOU *(~60 sec)*
- Heavy AI users have **9x more code churn** (code written then rewritten within 2 weeks)
- **4x more copy-paste duplication** — AI still struggles with DRY principles
- The risk: velocity looks great today, then the codebase quietly becomes harder to change
- Good news: the data shows devs *are* catching and fixing these issues — but only if they're looking

---

## WHAT TO ACTUALLY MEASURE *(~45 sec)*
- Don't just track output — track the trade-offs:
  - **Productivity:** PR cycle time, deployment frequency
  - **Quality:** Code churn rate, duplication, post-merge defects
  - **Efficiency ratio:** Output increase vs. review time increase
- You can't manage what you don't measure

---

## CLOSE — "Force Multiplier, Not Magic" *(~30 sec)*
- AI coding tools are real. 25% productivity lift, better test coverage, smoother reviews.
- But they're **force multipliers** — they amplify your team's existing strengths *and* weaknesses
- The teams that win aren't the ones who adopt fastest. They're the ones who **measure right.**
- *"The question isn't whether AI makes devs more productive. It does. The question is whether you're measuring the right things to make it last."*
